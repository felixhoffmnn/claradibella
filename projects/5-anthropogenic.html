Anthropogenic Narratives

Web design
Front-end development
Archive of Speculative Design
The Anthropogenic Narratives website archives 11 innovative Communication Design projects, exhibited during a show at the Triennale di Milano. These projects, developed through a process of didactic experimentation, are meant to provoke new questions and inspire reflection on our present and future, rather than offering clear-cut answers. The site invites users to explore these thought-provoking works in depth.
The name of the project is "Anthropogenic Narratives: Communicating and Experiencing Non-Human Perspectives”, which perfectly sums up the concept of the course and the exhibition: narrating from a non-anthropocentric point of view, putting nature at the center and showing through natural elements the consequences of climate change.
The identity is based on the effect of reaction diffusion, which is applied not only to the title of the exhibition but also to the various projects within the dedicated pages.
Visual identity as metaphor
The website we created is therefore not just a showcase, it embodies the concept of "looking through" to communicate from a new perspective allowing nature itself to become the main character and tell its own story.
